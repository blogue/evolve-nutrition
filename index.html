My Story

Alex McMahon, NTP

I graduated from Oregon State University with a BS in Human Development and Family Sciences with a focus in Early Childhood Education and a minor in Business in 2009, after which I began my career as a Preschool teacher.

Reading, researching and talking to people about nutrition became my passion, and I felt fortunate to have found it. I was lucky enough to be able to combine my passion for nutrition and my career as a preschool teacher by helping parents with their children’s eating habits, leading cooking lessons with the kids and teaching nutrition in the preschool.

Along the way I realized my passion for nutrition and helping others could become a career. I came upon the Nutritional Therapy Association and their Nutritional Therapy Practitioner (NTP) program, and I knew it was for me. Graduating from the Nutritional Therapy Practitioner provided me with the skills and knowledge to help others using holistic nutrition. Upon graduation, I founded Evolve Nutritional Therapy LLC.

Evolve Nutritional Therapy's Philosophy

Nutrition

Many modern health challenges are the result of imbalances and weaknesses in the body caused by poor nutrition and lifestyle factors. The human body is innately intelligent and has the power to heal and repair itself when given the proper nutrition and implementing a healthier lifestyle. Empower yourself with nutrition and take charge of your own health!

Sleep

Research suggests that being sleep deprived on a consistent basis significantly impairs psychomotor function, memory retrieval, work productivity, fat loss, muscle building, blood sugar regulation, food cravings, willpower and hormonal function. Getting eight to nine hours of quality restorative sleep is downright necessary to be happy healthy individuals who function at a high level.

Nutritious Movement

Our bodies are beautifully designed to move often and at varying degrees of intensity. Low intensity movements like hiking, yoga, walking, bike riding or snow shoeing are much different than higher intensity movements like sprinting, weight training, high intensity interval training or martial arts. Both high intensity and low intensity healthy movements have benefits and we need them both.

Stress Management

The studies of stress and the negative impacts on health in the past few years have been eye opening, which is why it can no longer be overlooked. At Evolve Nutritional Therapy I recognize that health spans much further than just nutrition and for that reason stress management is one of the pillars of my philosophy on holistic health.
